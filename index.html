Forensic Psychiatric Services

Competency Evaluation / Fitness to Proceed

Comprehensive assessment of a defendant's current mental status and ability to understand proceedings and assist counsel, in compliance with Dusky v. United States standards. Available for both defense and prosecution.

Mental State Evaluation / Criminal Responsibility

Retrospective psychiatric evaluation of a defendant's mental state at the time of the alleged offense, including Not Guilty by Reason of Insanity (NGRI) assessments and diminished capacity analyses.

Sentence Mitigation

Expert evaluation and testimony on psychiatric diagnoses, developmental history, trauma, and neurocognitive factors relevant to sentencing, including capital cases and enhanced sentencing considerations.

Psychiatric Evaluation / Diagnosis Clarification

Independent diagnostic evaluation to clarify or challenge existing psychiatric diagnoses in the context of litigation, disability, or court-ordered assessment.

Record Review

Systematic review of medical, psychiatric, and correctional records to identify clinically significant findings and provide opinions relevant to legal proceedings.

Treatment Recommendations

Evidence-based psychiatric treatment recommendations for restoration to competency, court-ordered treatment compliance, and ongoing forensic monitoring in inpatient or community settings.

Background & Qualifications

About Dr. Bemporad

Dr. Bemporad brings extensive forensic psychiatry experience developed as the sole full-time psychiatrist for Montana's 54-bed forensic inpatient unit. He is board certified by the American Board of Psychiatry and Neurology (ABPN) and licensed to practice in Colorado, Montana, and New York.

He has authored or coauthored more than 500 forensic evaluations addressing competency to stand trial (fitness to proceed), criminal responsibility, mental state at the time of offense, and sentencing mitigation. He has provided expert testimony in nearly every district court across Montana and is experienced in communicating complex psychiatric findings in clear, objective, and legally relevant terms.

His background includes serving as a Forensic Review Board voting member, Chair of the Involuntary Medication Review Boards for both Montana State Hospital and the Montana State Prison, and former President of the Medical Staff of Montana State Hospital. Dr. Bemporad has presented on forensic topics to legislative commissions and state leadership, testified in over 20 Sell hearings, and routinely collaborates with attorneys, courts, and state agencies to provide thorough psychiatric consultation.
